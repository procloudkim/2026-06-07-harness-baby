# MAS Decisions

## Round 1 - Visionary

BabyGear Risk Radar should feel like a reusable Codex decision room. The value is not another product-safety article; it is a repeatable workflow that turns uncertainty into a structured parent action card.

## Round 2 - Critical

The biggest risk is overclaiming. A baby-product workflow can accidentally convert a disputed public article into a safety verdict. The skill must force source tiers, uncertainty, and "not medical advice" language before alternatives.

## Round 3 - Visionary

The plugin should make brand trust auditable. If a parent says "I trusted this famous brand," Codex should respond with a traceable map: who said what, how strong the evidence is, what is unknown, and what a parent can check next.

## Round 4 - Critical

Hooks and MCP would add complexity and trust-review friction. For a 90-minute submission, a manifest, skill, examples, screenshot, validator, and reports are more reliable and easier to inspect.

## Round 5 - Visionary

MAS personas make the output more parent-centered: one lens keeps the lived reality visible, another checks pediatric safety boundaries, another translates into action, and a QA lead catches missing citations.

## Round 6 - Critical

The YouTube channels should not become data sources. The plugin should not scrape, quote, or imply endorsement. They are abstract persona anchors only.

## Current Synthesis

Build a root-level Codex plugin package with no hooks/MCP/apps. Optimize for clear evidence handling, source governance, parent-safe wording, and repeatable PowerShell validation.
